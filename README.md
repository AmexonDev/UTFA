# European Touch Football Association Regulations 
##### Last Updated: February 28, 2026
--------------------------------------------------
## Section 1 - Gameplay
### Team Requirements
##### 1.0 Each team must have a minimum of 3 players present to begin a match.
##### 1.1 If one team has 3 players and the other has 4, the match will be played as a 4vs3.
##### 1.2 If a team fails to meet the minimum requirement of 3 players, a forfeit win is awarded to the opposing team.
##### 1.3 If both teams fail to meet the minimum requirement in a LEAGUE match, the result is a forfeit draw and a strike is given to both team managers.
##### 1.4 If both teams fail to meet the minimum requirement in a CUP match, the match will be rescheduled and a strike is given to both team managers.
##### 1.5 If a team falls below 3 players during a game, the game will still continue.
##### 1.6 If a player disconnects or leaves during a game, they will receive a verbal warning, followed by a yellow card if repeated.
##### 1.7 A player who disconnects or leaves during a game is allowed to rejoin or be replaced by a registered substitute.
##### 1.8 If the team remains below 3 players by the start of the next game, a forfeit win is awarded to the opposing team and the manager will be striked.
### Forfeit Wins
##### 1.9 A forfeit win is recorded as 2-0.
##### 1.10 A forfeit draw is recorded as 0-0.
##### 1.11 Forfeiting a game will result in a mandatory team vote to sack the manager.
### Ties
##### 1.12 If a cup fixture ends in a tie, the teams will play 2 additional games. This process will repeat until a winner is determined.
##### 1.13 The ‘Sudden Death’ in-game feature is not counted in matches, any goals scored during Sudden Death are voided.
##### 1.14 In CUP matches, Sudden Death is enforced if the aggregate score is tied on the last game.
##### 1.15 If the result remains tied, the process will repeat until a winner is determined.
### Game Requirements
##### 1.16 Games are required to have a referee present.
##### 1.17 If a referee is unavailable, the match may be delayed or rescheduled by mutual agreement of both managers.
##### 1.18 Referees are prohibited from officiating matches involving their own team.
##### 1.19 Each team has the right to host one game.
##### 1.20 Hosting order is determined by a coin flip conducted via a Discord Automated Bot (e.g. carlbot). The winner may choose to host the first or second game.
##### 1.21 After the first game ends, the second game will commence and the non-hosting team will host.
##### 1.22 If the hosting team cannot provide a private server 5 minutes after the coinflip, the referee or the opposing team will provide one. The original hosting team will still host the game.
Substitutions
##### 1.23 Substitutions may only be made when the game is paused after a goal is scored, or if the referee pauses the game.
##### 1.24 If a game is paused for any reason, it will only play the remaining time when resumed.
##### 1.25 Players who are substituted out can only re-enter later in the match if their team falls under 4 players in the server.
##### 1.26 Substitutions can only be made by the manager (or assistant if the manager is not present).
