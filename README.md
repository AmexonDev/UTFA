# UTFA League Rules
### The purpose of this document is to outline the rules and boundaries of Polish Touch Football Association for the game Touch Football.
### Updated: September 16th, 2025
----------------------------------------------------
## Section A | Gameplay
### 1. All matches must have at least three players ready for each team
**a.** In the event that one opposing team has three players ready while another opposing team has all four, the referee will force a 4v3
match
**b.** In the event that one opposing team has two players ready while
another opposing team has all three, both teams need to agree in
order for a 3v2 to happen. Otherwise, it will be an forfeit win
against the shorter-staffed team
**c.** If a team fails to meet this criterion, then the opposing team will take the forfeit win
-# **i.** If both teams fail to meet this criterion, then both teams will take a forfeit draw. Only applicable in a league match.
-# **ii.** A forfeit win will be listed as a 4-0 win.
**d. In cup matches, if both teams fail to show up, then an option to reschedule within the same weekend can be made.**
**e.** If a team goes below the required three players threshold during the match, the affected leg of the match will continue until the next leg, to which the match comes to a pause.
-# **i.** If a team fails to meet the required three players threshold by one unofficial leg, then the team will automatically receive a forfeit loss of all remaining legs, not including the leg of incident.
### 2. Once a match result has been met, the result cannot be changed
**a.** There are exceptions in which the League Administration is involved under case-by-case situations, but this rule generally applies to all normally conducted matches.
### 3. All matches will have up to two legs of gameplay
**a.** In these two legs of gameplay, the Sudden Death game mechanic will not count, and all goals that are gained at the time of Sudden Death are disallowed
-# **i.** In Cup matches, Sudden Death is allowed and enforced.
-# **ii.** If a cup match goes to a tie, then two more legs (using the cup mode) will be played, one at the current host, another either at current host or another host (dependent on what the non-hosting party decides)
-# 1. If a cup match still goes to a tie, then the processrepeats until doesn’t result in a draw at the end of thegiven two legs
### 4. All games must have a referee present
**a.** In the event that a referee is not present during the game, the match will be subject to possibility of delay. A reschedule can be made as a result if both managers agree.
**b.** Referees are discouraged from reffing their own matches
-# **i.** In a **last-case** scenario, referees can ref their own matches if they aren’t playing the match
### 5. Each team has the opportunity to host one leg
**a.** To determine the hosting order, the referee will ask the managers/assistants of both teams “Heads or tails?”, with the first answer posted taking priority. The referee will then use a Discord Automated Bot to make a coinflip, which outputs a result. The manager with the matching coin side has the option to choose between hosting the First leg or Last leg. **Legs are hosted by using a private server link to the Touch Football game of the hosting party’s choice.**
-# **i.** If the hosting team is unable to provide a private server link before the FFT mark, or if the team hosting team doesn’t have a private server link, then the ref or the other team will provide a private server link, giving the hosting team a guaranteed opportunity to host.
**b.** After the first two legs, the non-hosting team has the option to “switch hosts”, as in changing the previous host into their host
**c.** The non-hosting team has the right to keep the current host after the first leg
### 6. Teams are allowed to make substitutions at any point of time, as long as the substitution does not directly interfere with the match.
**a.** As a result, substitutions can be made during an official leg
**b.** Teams are allowed to substitute back players that have been substituted out of the match before
**c.** Substitutions have to be sanctioned by the Manager/Assistant.
### 7. In a private server, only the players playing are allowed in the
private server
**a.** In other words, no substitutes are allowed in the match specific private server
**b.** Referees and streamers sanctioned to their match can be in the match-specific private server
### 8. If a team fields an ineligible player for an entire leg, the opposing team will gain +4 goals for each leg violated
**a.** Ineligible players are defined as players that should not be playing within the league. See Section A, Rule 10.
### 9. If a player scores a goal that seems impossible/unpredictable, the goal will be reviewed by referees and VAR (recording technology) on a case-by-case basis.
**a. Players are ENCOURAGED to clip any goal that seems to meet this criteria**
**b.** If a goal is determined to satisfy this criteria, then the goal is
subject to dismissal
**c.** If there is no recollection of proof, then the goal will stand.
**d.** In any situation that involves goal line specific goals, recording of the match is needed and will be examined for review.
**e. VAR (recording technology) is only applicable in goals where THE GAME HAS COUNTED THE GOAL.**
### 10. There are limitations which constitute a player to become ineligible
**a.** The player has to be playing on their main account, in respect to Roblox AND Discord.
-# **i.** Exceptions can be made. However, the player has to contact a Board of Director/Administrator as to why their main account isn't used (THIS IS ROBLOX SPECIFIC ONLY, WITH THE ONLY EXCEPTION BEING DISABLED/BANNED ACCOUNTS ON DISCORD)
**b.** A team CANNOT field a player that is currently gamebanned
-# **i.** Gamebans are defined as bans in which the player CANNOT play at any official match. Gamebans apply to ALL LEAGUE AND CUP GAMES
-# 1. Gamebans are set by a duration of how many games the player is disbarred from. This includes League and Cup games in which the player’s parent team is currently a participant in
**c.** A team CANNOT field a player that is suspected to be an Alternate Account
-# **i.** Alternate accounts are defined as accounts that deviate from the player’s main account/identity
**d.** A team CANNOT field a player that is suspected to be a Shared
Account
-# **i.** Shared accounts are defined as accounts linked to more than one owner, with no real personal identifiers
**e**. A team CANNOT field a player that has been found to be
exploiting/using hacks in the game
-# **i.** Exploiting/hacks are defined as modifications to the game that are impossible to legitimately achieve without using game modifiers
-# **ii.** Exploiting in a match results in a ban
**f.** In Cup Matches, A team CANNOT field a player that has been cup-tied.
-# **i.** A cup-tied player can be defined as a player that has lost their match, no matter if they played in the match or not.
-# **ii.** Fielding a cup-tied player in a cup match will results
-# **iii.** If UTFA Administration fails to give a Cup-Tied role to a cup-tied player, then Administration will take responsibility, and a rematch can be played.
### 11. Match fixing is not allowed
**a.** Match fixing is defined as playing unfairly to get a predetermined result for personal gain
-# **i.** An example of match fixing is intentionally losing a match/playing poorly in order to obtain a match result for personal gain.
### 12. In the event that a Manager and Assistant Manager is unavailable for a match, then the responsibility of Managers/Assistant Managers in a game will be shifted to a volunteering player for that match.
**a.** The player with that shift of responsibility can’t make any signings, nor make any scheduling changes. They can only control substitutions and coin flips.
### 13. If a team fails to show up to their game with the necessary amount of players, than the Manager will be given a Manager Strike
**a.** If a manager reaches two manager strikes, then the manager will be forcefully sacked by the League.
### 14. If an ineligible player is caught playing a league game, the match will result in a forfeit win to the non-alting team.
**a.** This includes alternate accounts and account sharing
### 15. If an ineligible player is caught playing a league game, the outcome can result in a replay OR forfeit
**a.** This include alternate accounts and account sharing
-# **i.** If the manager was aware of the infringement and let the account play, it will result in a forfeit for manager misconduct.
-# **ii.** If players on the team knew about the infringement, it would be forfeit.
**b.** If a player account shared without the team knowing, a replay may be issued.
### 16. If any match has complications due to staff error, a replay or fft will be made, depending on the case
### 17. Game rules are subject to change at all times

## Section B | League
### 1. Teams are limited to a maximum of 11 players, including the Manager and the Assistant Manager
**a.** If the player count exceeds the maximum limit, then the League will ask the manager to make a release, or force a release by themselves
### 2. In a contract, the manager is required to outline the Duration and Release Clause to the player
**a.** Managers need proof that the player accepted the contract’s terms and conditions
**b.** Contracts can include a “No-Jail Clause”, which allows the player to leave at any point of their contract. This clause is recognized by the league.
**c.** Release Clauses are defined as a set price that when activated, automatically allows the player to sign for another team without interference from the parent team
-# **i.** Release clauses cannot be activated when the Transfer Market is currently closed. See Section B, Rule 4a and 4b
**d.** In contracts, there are options to either sign a player by a full season (1 season), sign a player by many seasons (2 seasons), or sign a player by the amount of games
-# **i.** When signing players by a season, players are contracted to the ENTIRETY of the season, including pre-season cups, and postseason cups
### 3. Managers have the right to keep players “jailed”, even if the player has intentions to leave their contract early
### 4. In UTFA, the league uses a Transfer Market System
**a.** The Transfer Market is not always open. Opening of the Transfer Market is dependent on the dates when markets open. Transfer Markets can be closed, with the actions of the Transfer Market being void/unrecognized by the league.
**b.** In the Transfer Market, the following actions are locked into the Transfer Market
-# **i.** Buying/Selling of players: With the approval of both teams + the respective player(s), teams have the opportunity to buy and sell players with a fee
-# 1. Contracts terms still need to be met, see Section B, Rule 2
-# **ii.** Loaning of players: With the approval of both teams + the respective player(s), teams have the opportunity to loan players with an allotted duration
-# 1. For each duration, the paying team has to pay a fee PER GAME. Teams determine the cumulative fee to pay, which will be split by how many games are played. 
-# 2. If a loan is still ongoing after a closure of the Transfer Market, then the loan is allowed to run its course, with no extension
-# 3. Loans cannot be extended after terms and conditions have been made
-# **iii.** Activation of Release Clauses: See Section B, Rule 2c
-# **iv.** Trading of players: With the approval of both teams + the respective player(s), teams have the opportunity to trade players with no required fees
-# 1, Fees can still be added on, but the contract has to explicitly state which team is paying for which fee
**c.** The following actions are NOT locked into the transfer market
-# **i.** Signing of players: Managers have the right to sign Free Agents at any given point
-# 1. Free Agents are defined as players with no contractual obligations to a team in UTFA.
-# 2, If a player signs for another team, signings have to be done AFTER a release has been made for a respective player. No exceptions
-# 3, Signings have to be made 30 minutes BEFORE a match’s scheduled time. Any signing past the 30 minute mark will be voided.
-# 4. Signings CANNOT be done at just one game. **A minimum of four games is required.**
-# **ii.** Releasing of players: Managers have the right to release players at any given point after 7 days since the player has been signed
-# 1. Players HAVE to stay in their team for at least 7 days before a release can be made
-# **iii.** Extension of contracts: Managers have the right to extend contracts or change clauses
-# 1. Contract extensions HAVE TO be explicit in any changes. Clauses that were once initially agreed on are REQUIRED to be addressed in a contract extension. This includes Release Clauses and “No-Jail Clauses”.
-# **iv.** Disbanding of a team: Managers have the right to disband their team **UNDER ONE CONDITION**
-# 1. Managers looking to disband their team **HAVE TO HAVE MANAGED THEIR TEAM FOR NINE LEAGUE GAMEWEEKS**|
**d.** All Transfer Market decisions involving the payment of fees requires the budget to remain above or at 0€ after payment processing
-# **i.** If a team goes below 0€, then they cannot buy or loan players. They are only allowed to sell, trade (without fee to pay), release, or sign players
### 5. Managers are allowed to leave their Managerial spot, as long as they are able to find a suitable replacement, and three weeks have passed since their arrival (21 days)
**a.** Suitable replacements are limited to signed players and the Assistant Manager. Managers CANNOT appoint outside of their team
-# **i.** In the event that the Manager does want to appoint outside of the parent team, then the Manager needs to sign the player, then step down from their position for the new manager to either stay in their club, or allowed the manager to release the former manager
**b.** In the event that a Manager leaves the league, a replacement will take place. Priority will be shifted to the players of said team
-# **i.** If the players of said team are unable to reach an agreement on who should be appointed manager, then the League will exercise to right to open the managerial spot to any Free Agent willing and capable
### 6. Players are NOT allowed to play for more than one game in one gameweek
**a.** Contracts that attempt to bypass this rule will be voided
### 7. In UTFA, gamebans can be provided against players in violation of specific rules
**a.** If a player has been found to have used an alternate account/account shared in an official match, then they will receive **6 gamebans per league match, 9 gamebans per cup match.**
### 8. If it is proven that a Manager, Assistant Manager, or player of the offending team were aware of fielding an Alternate/Shared Account (see Section A, Rule 10c + 10d), a FORFEIT LOSS will be made against the parent team, depending on the number of games the player has been fielded.
**a.** If neither the Manager, Assistant Managers, or any player in the parent team had any prior knowledge to the Alternate/Shared account, then a replay can be made, depending on the situation.
**b.** Managers/Assistant Managers who were aware of any Alternate/Shared accounts in their team will be met with a Seasonal Manager Ban
**c.** **In cup matches, if Section B, Rule 8 is applicable, the offending team will FORFEIT their previous cup match.**
### 9. Managers are NOT allowed to take bribes/payments outside of the transfer system of any kind for transfers
**a.** This includes **offers** of any outside payments
-# **i.** Example: A recruitment message advertising nitro payments for joining
**b.** Nitro, Robux, Gift cards, Real life payments, etc., are disallowed payments for all actions in the Transfer Market System
-# **i.** Managers/Assistant Managers who gave bribes/payments will receive a Seasonal Manager Ban
### 10. In order to play for the league, your Discord Account has to be at least 3 months old
**a.** If your account was made because of your main account getting disabled, then this is an exception. Let an admin know.
### 11. A player can initiate a sacking against their manager after 21 days of their official ppointment
**a.** Contracts/Transfers/Releases made the day when the sacking with announced will be revoked.
**b.** A simple majority is needed for a sacking attempt to be successful
-# **i.** A simple majority is defined by a majority of players (more than 50%) agreeing.
**c.** With the 21D Sack Rule, players who vote or initiate a sacking are not able to be released without permission from a Board of Director+. This is to prevent teams from collapsing.
### 12. If a player leaves the league, and does not come back two weeks after their departure, then they are automatically released
**a.** This does not include players who had their account disabled
**b.** In order to point out a specific case of a player’s departure, make a ticket in UTFA, or talk to a staff member.
### 13. League rules are subject to change at all times

**a.** Even if you haven’t broken a rule in the league specifically, if it is proven that you have broken a serious rule that applies to this league, and that an imminent threat can be reasonably expected out of yourself by the community, then you are subject to a Community Ban

**a.** The UTFA President has the final say to all decisions. Whatever position the UTFA President takes in a decision is the final say for the league
**b.** The power structure follows in ascending (minimum -> maximum) order:
-# **i.** Referee -> Moderator -> Administrator -> Board of Directors -> Vice President -> President
